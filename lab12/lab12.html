Planets Slideshow

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. Its orbit around the Sun takes 87.97 Earth days, the shortest of all the planets in the Solar System. It is named after the Greek god Hermes, translated into Latin as Mercurius.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. As the second brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon, Venus can cast shadows and can sometimes be visible in daylight.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. About 29 percent of Earth's surface is land, while the rest is covered with water.

Mars

Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere. Its surface features include impact craters, valleys, deserts, and polar ice caps.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant and one of the brightest objects visible in the night sky.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest in the Solar System. It is best known for its large and beautiful ring system.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It has the third largest planetary radius in the Solar System and is named after the Greek god of the sky.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun. It is a giant planet and is slightly more massive than Uranus.
